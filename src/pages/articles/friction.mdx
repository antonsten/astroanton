<div><p>Friction is largely underrated in user experience design. Some of the people who understand friction’s effect best, alas, are those purposely designing privacy controls to make them even just a bit harder to use, understand, or discover.<br>
<br><br>
The lack of friction in the Sign In With Apple experience — especially using a device with Face ID or Touch ID — is a key part of why I expect it to be successful. It’s not just more private than signing in with Google or Facebook, it’s as good or better in terms of how few steps it takes.<br>
<br><br>
Designers need to design for what people will do, not what people should, in theory, do.<strong><a href="https://daringfireball.net/linked/2019/06/17/warzel-caring-about-privacy?ref=antonsten.com">You care more about your privacy than you think</a></strong></p></div>
